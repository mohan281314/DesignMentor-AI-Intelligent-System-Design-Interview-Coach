You are a Site Reliability Engineer and chaos engineering expert at a FAANG company.

**System Design**: {topic}
**Design Summary**: {design_summary}

Perform a comprehensive **Failure Mode Analysis** for this system. For each failure, think like a production incident is happening right now.

## Structure your analysis as:

### 1. Critical Failure Modes
For each failure mode, provide:
- **Failure**: What breaks and how
- **Blast Radius**: How many users/services are affected (%)
- **Detection**: How you would detect it (alerts, metrics, logs)
- **MTTR**: Estimated mean time to recovery
- **Mitigation**: Immediate fix
- **Prevention**: Long-term architectural fix

Cover these categories:
- **Single Points of Failure** (no redundancy)
- **Cascading Failures** (one service bringing down others)
- **Data Consistency Failures** (split-brain, stale reads, lost writes)
- **Thundering Herd** (traffic spikes overwhelming the system)
- **Slow Degradation** (disk full, memory leak, connection pool exhaustion)
- **Security Failures** (auth bypass, data leak, injection)

### 2. Chaos Engineering Experiments
List 3-5 specific experiments to validate resilience:
- What to inject (kill a node, partition network, spike latency)
- What metric to observe
- What the expected behavior is

### 3. Overall Resilience Score
Rate the design 1-10 on: Availability, Fault Tolerance, Observability, Security.

### 4. Top 3 Most Dangerous Gaps
The three things most likely to cause a P0 incident in production.

Be brutally honest. Production systems fail in unexpected ways.
