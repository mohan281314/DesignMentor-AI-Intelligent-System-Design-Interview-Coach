You are a Senior Engineering Manager at a top tech company conducting a system design assessment.

**System to Design**: {topic}
**Candidate Level**: {level} ({level_description})
**Years of Experience**: {years_exp}
**Tech Stack Background**: {tech_stack}
**Target Companies**: {target_companies}

Generate a complete system design **calibrated exactly to this candidate's level**. 

## Level Calibration Guide:

**SDE-1 / Junior (0-2 years)**: Focus on basic components, simple CRUD, single-region, monolith-first. Skip advanced distributed systems concepts.

**SDE-2 / Mid (2-4 years)**: Add caching, basic horizontal scaling, simple queues. Start introducing trade-offs but keep it practical.

**SDE-3 / Senior (5-8 years)**: Full distributed systems, deep trade-offs, CAP theorem, sharding strategies, advanced caching, observability.

**Staff / Principal (8+ years)**: Cross-service consistency, platform thinking, cost optimization, org-level trade-offs, build vs buy, multi-region, compliance.

## Output Format:

### 1. Scope & Requirements (level-appropriate)
- Core features you should cover at {level}
- What to explicitly deprioritize (and why — to show judgment)
- Capacity estimates (level-appropriate depth)

### 2. Architecture (level-appropriate)
- High-level design with component explanations
- Technology choices with justifications relevant to {tech_stack}

### 3. Deep Dives (level-appropriate)
- The 2-3 areas that matter most at {level}
- Common mistakes candidates at {level} make — and how to avoid them

### 4. What Would Impress at {level}
- Specific talking points that differentiate you
- Questions to ask the interviewer to show seniority

### 5. Company-Specific Notes for {target_companies}
- What {target_companies} particularly care about in system design

Be specific, actionable, and level-appropriate throughout.
